Overview

Pokémon Hyokyo (temporary name) is a Pokémon Fan game based on the Pokémon games and animes. I started co-developing this project in February 2024. It is developed using RPG Maker XP and the Ruby language. The game is currently being developed in English, but it is planned that it will also be available in French in version 1.0.

We, the two developers, have played all the major Pokémon games, from Pokémon Yelllow, when we were five, to the latest DLC of Pokémon Scarlet/Violet, at the end of 2023. When we played the Pokemon Scarlet and Violet DLCs, we reflected on the whole series and how it evolved. As we began to point out the shortcomings of each game, we started to discuss what the perfect Pokemon game would look like. What kind of game would we have killed to have when we were kids? Even though we had some amazing Pokémon games when we were young (Pokémon Gold/Silver and their remake Pokémon HeartGold and SoulSilver are, to this day, the best and most complete Pokémon games I have ever played), we didn't see their flaws at the time. Now that we are older, it is something we can do something about. That is why we decided to launch this project.

What do we mean by "flaws"? This is just our opinion on the matter, and it does not take anything away from the original Pokémon games which has produced great games for several decades. However, I am going to go through a few of them to give you an idea of what we are trying to put together in this project:

  • A thousand and some Pokémon? Are you crazy? How many are forgettable? Most of the time, more Pokémon adds nothing to the game other than superficial content. Let's just keep the best ones (in our opinion, sorry if your favourite did not make the cut).
  • Remember that amazing feature that was present in one game and disappeared in the next? Let's bring it back.
  • Legendaries who are more common than your starter. Nope, that is not going to happen. You want it, go and get it, we willmake it difficult for you.
  • I cannot start the game with my favorite Pokémon. Now you, most likely, will.
  • The game can be beaten by a five-year-old with a single (unoptimised) Pokémon without any game over. Yes, it is about time to tackle this one too.
  • A linear story line or a shallow, empty open world, the choice is yours (sometimes both, unfortunately). Let's give you a real open world filled with things to do.
  • Stupid features like: Do I need a friend to complete my Pokédex? No, you won't.
  • In this game, let's introduce a forgettable new fighting gimmick that was, somehow, previously unknown! Nope, nope, and let's keep those gimmicks to a minimum.
  • The story goes like this: I live with my mum (dad is missing, obviously), and I go on an adventure with my classmates. Everyone is nice to me, except for the eccentric budget Team Rocket, who does evil (not that evil) things for the sake of being evil. Along the way, I will be the chosen one, because nobody is interested in you if you are not special. I will be catching Legendaries faster than I can eat oreos out of the packet, and discovering a whole shitload of previously unknown but obvious facts about the Pokémon world, which will make me wonder if other people have eyes. I will roll over everyone to become league champion, which is, somehow, considered an impressive achievement in the Pokémon world. Not interested? We agree, let's play a more mature game, we are not eight years old any more.
  • Finally, one of the main shortcomings of the latest Pokémon games was the lack of connection between players and their Pokémons. To be honest, I cannot even remember a single Pokémon from my team in Pokémon Sword and Shield. Why is that? There are too many Pokémons, few if any quests that put Pokémon at the centre of the story (with the exception of legendary Pokémon), and above all, in a way, your Pokémons do not matter.

I will go into more detail on each flaw, and more, in the dedicated section where we try to correct them. Overall, this project aims to bring players into the world of Pokémon.

It is an adventure with YOUR Pokémon. We want to bring back the feeling that was most present in the first games and in the anime. If you do not understand what I mean, try to imagine what Ash would look like without his Pikachu. Or if you ditched your starter in the first three generations of Pokémon games. You could have done it. I know I never did, and I have played them over and over again. Why? Because they mattered to me. The first thing I did in Pokémon Scarlet and Violet was to ditch my starter for a weaker Psyduck. I am not proud of it, but I could not care less about him. Bringing that feeling back will undoubtedly be the most challenging aspect of this project, but we will give it our best shot.

Not everything in this game will be legendary, unknown or unseen. The main character is not that special, and, has not discovered any Pokémon, sorry. If there's one sentence that could sum up this game, it would be this: The Pokémon world, lore and games already have everything they need, they just need to be put together properly. The game will, hopefully, be closer to the anime story wise (minus the constant Team Rocket gimmicks). The main focus will be on exploring the Hyokyo region, discovering stories about Pokémon and people, and training your Pokémons (or not).

For this project, we wanted to create all the extra features, art and stories ourselves. However, I have learned in the past that this is not substainable as this is, still, a small team project. So we decided to start the project with Pokémon Essential (v21), which is a preset for RPG Maker XP that lets you create Pokémon fan games. In addition, we will also be using some of the plugins created by the incredible community of Pokémon fans where appropriate. In this article, I will always mention cases where a feature, which has been added to the game, was created, even partially, by someone else. If there is no mention of it and it is not in the Pokémon series, we have done it ourselves. In addition, the Pokémon Compagny deserves much of the credit for the game's artworks, musics and lore, obviously.

The Region of Hyokyo

In this section, I will be presenting the Hyokyo region in which this Pokémon Fan Game takes place. I will be presenting the world map, the Pokédex (i.e. the Pokémon that can be found in Hyokyo) and the starters (i.e. the pool from which players can choose their first Pokémon).





The World Map

For the world map, we have tried to reach the level of the Pokémon Ruby/Sapphire/Emerauld map, which we think is almost perfect. Why almost perfect? It managed to include different biomes in a meaningful and fluid way. What's more, the developers at the time managed to make every road, cave and mountain unique. Only the water routes feel similar, but even here there are specific features that make them unique in the Hoenn region.

In designing the map, we therefore decided to include a variety of biomes and try to link them together in a somewhat realistic way.



  • In the centre of the map, you will find plains and forests.
  • In the centre-west lies the Al-Kahla desert.
  • To the north-west are mountains, caves and the Mount Igneous volcano.
  • To the south-west lies a dense jungle.
  • To the south-west, on the water, is a group of atolls.
  • To the south, an island formed by a meteorite impact and the deep water zone.
  • To the south-east, is an archipelago of ancient volcanic islands.
  • In the ceentre-east, is a swampy area.
  • To the east is an area of dense forest.
  • To the north-east, there are caves and high snow-capped mountains.
  • To the north, there are low-lying mountains, caves and a mountain lake.

However, compared to the Hoenn region, we have decided to draw a land region, instead of a sort of island. We felt that such a diversity of biomes would be difficult to find realistically on an island. In addition, a number of islands are already part of the region itself.

When we placed all the Pokémon in the region, we took care to place them in the ecosystem to which they belong. We wanted the world to feel alive and waiting for the player to discover it. As a result, you may have trouble finding Fire Pokémon in forests or near grass. In the Pokémon game, only one or two types of Pokémon of a specific lineage are usually found in the wild on a given route. The reason for this is that the game has been designed in a linear fashion, and it would not make much sense to have the player face a random level 45 Raticac right after the first town. To deal with this problem, the routes in the Pokémon games franchise are mainly level areas. Compared to the Pokémon franchise, in the Hyokyo region you can find all the Pokémon in their respective biomes, with the exception of non-roaming legendaries. This way, one really gets the impression that the Pokémon live and evolve there. I refer you to the Overworld Pokémons section if you are interested in how we can make this work in terms of game functionality.

As the game is supposed to be an open world, we have designed the paths to allow the player to go pretty much anywhere in any order they want. The player starts the game in Nishitsu, in the centre of the map, at the eastern end of the Al-Kahla desert. From there, and after the introduction, they can go wherever they want, no path is blocked. The underlying idea was that we did not want the player to feel entitled to follow a predetermined path. This may be more difficult to put in place, but it has a few of advantages:

  • It enhances the player's immersion and sense of belonging to a connected and living world.
  • This increases replayability and the feeling of discovering the region in your own way.

It comes with a number of challenges to balance the difficulty of wild Pokémon and trainers. I refer you to the Level Scaling section if you are interested in how we tackle this problem.

As in the Hoenn region, each road, cave and mountain is designed to be unique. Each has its own unique design, atmosphere, climate, quests, secrets and Pokémon. This is a very important point for us, because we want every route to be worth visiting and exploring.

Unlike most Pokémon games, which guide the player through the region, unlocking new paths as the story unfolds, here we aim to give the player more freedom. Of course, some quests will require you to go to a specific location. But players will not have to wait for the story to progress before getting there. Some secondary paths may still be blocked by a specific ability or story, but no town will be out of reach because of the main quests. The idea behind it is simple: if so many other people have managed to reach such a place in a fairly well-mapped region, why should not you? Hopefully, that this will result in a more credible and organic world, while giving the player real freedom.

The Pokédex

The Hyokyo region has 617 Pokémons. It includes 45 Pokémon forms (Galar, Alolan, Paldean and Hisuian) and 30 Legendary Pokémons.

The way we chose the Pokémons was simple: we chose the ones we liked, and discarded the ones we did not like. We did this bearing in mind that we had to fill an entire region with various biomes with Pokémons, obviously.

The Pokédex therefore contains most of the first three generations of Pokémon (Kanto, Johto and Hoenn), as well as their forms. We have added all the good ones, in our opinion, in subsequent generations. This leaves us with almost 50% of the grand total of around 1,300 Pokémons available. To be honest, it is more than I expected, and it is a credit to the artists and designers of the Pokémon games that they have managed to get such a variety of good and great Pokémons.

We thought that players should be able to fill their Pokédex and obtain all the Pokémon on their own. We understood the commercial value of hiding a few Pokémons behind a trading mechanism. However, we are not going to make any money out of it, and we do not think a forced trading feature is the best kind of multiplayer feature this franchise has to offer. We have therefore decided to remove this obstacle. All Pokémon that evolve through trade have been reworked to evolve through leveling up or a stone mechanism.

While it was creatively tempting to create and add our own Pokémons to this Pokédex, we really felt that Pokémon games do not need Pokémon additions to be great games. This game is in fact a proof of concept of this idea. Already with 'From 1 AU', which we developed, we had no intention of adding new features just for the sake of adding new things. We do not believe that new means better, and that is simply not the way we create and design games. So we have decided to limit ourselves to Pokémons that already exist.

The Starters

We always thought it was a shame not to let the player choose which pokémon to start their journey with. It's true that classic starter Pokémons are generally more charismatic and better than others. However, in my case, there is no way I am doing a playthrough without my Gengar. I might as well get a Gastly as a starter, if I wanted to. Of course, I would likely have a hard time at the start of the game against just about anyone, because Gastly is pretty weak and only knows Lick for a good part of the early game. But that is precisely why it would never occur to me to ditche such a starter: we have been through this together.

This also touches on a point I made earlier: not everything has to be legendary or unknown. Your favourite Pokémon is a Cartepie, and you want to start the game with it, so you should be able to. Why, in every Pokémon game, do you have to be the chosen one, constantly hit by insulting luck? In most Pokémon games you are usually the best throughout the whole run, which is not very challenging. I prefer to immerse myself in a story where you're nothing special and have humble beginnings, but work your way to the top, IF you want to: that's what life is like for most of us.

In our opinion, this freedom in the choice of starter not only strengthens the bond between the player and their first Pokémon, but also offers a wide variety of playthroughs and play styles. For example, if I start with a Gastly, it is very unlikely, or at least quite a challenge, to take care of the normal gym first (the one in the starting town).

In total, there are 208 possible starters in this game. The only requirements for a Pokémon to be a starter were that not to be a Legendary and to be the first Pokémon in a Pokémon line (i.e. have at least one evolution). We hve decided to reserve starter status for those who have at least one evolution, as this is your first Pokémon and you will most likely have it for the whole game. It could therefore be important for players to feel some form of evolution in their Pokémon, and this could be achieved through the evolution of the starter into another form. It is debatable whether we should have included other Pokémon in the the starter pool, but that is what we chose to do.

Art

Before delving into the arts we have developed for this game, I need to talk about a few of the game's specifications:

  • The resolution of the game is 512x384 pixels.
  • The sprites and artworks have a 2-pixels unit, which means that what appears to be a pixel on the screen is in fact a 2x2 pixel square.

It is this pixel unit that gives the game its GameBoy Advance old-fashioned look. Because we want to keep this look for the game, we have made sure that all the items and sprites in the game respect this 2-pixels unit. However, such a unit results in a rather crude and angular user interface (UI). We therefore decided to use a 1-pixel unit for the UIs to modernise them somewhat.

We mainly use a simple software programme, "paint.net", to create all the artworks presented in this section.





The World Map Design

For the world map interface, we have opted for a Pokémon HeartGold/SoulSilver style, which we feel perfectly embodies the atmosphere of the Pokémon games on Gameboy Advance and early Nintendo DS. It is simple and easy to read, but also stylish and pleasing to the eye.

There is not much else to say here. It took few days to get the design right, but in the end we are pretty happy with the result.

The Recipe Book UI

To achieve this, the UI wanted it to be a notebook in which players would write down the recipes they received. In this case, we have taken inspiration from the style of the Pokédex in Pokémon Legend Arceus. We thought this UI was quite effective and matched the style we were looking for.

We have redesigned the UI (top left gif) to match the screen resolution and theme of the game. The UI has been coded and designed largely from scratch, but uses small pieces of code taken from the "Modular Pause Menu" plugin by Luka S.J. and A.I.R, mainly for reasons of consistency across the various UI codes.





The aim of The Recipe Book interface is to provide quick access to the recipes that the player knows. To ensure that this interface is effective, we have taken care to provide access to a range of information so that players can gather ressources and craft items efficiently:

  • All necessary ingredients.
  • The number of each ingredient required.
  • The number available in the player's bag.
  • A description of the corresponding craftable item.

In the same style, we have created a UI for meal preparation (image bottom left), which mainly involves choosing the berries the player wants in their meal. I refer you to The Crafting and Recipe System feature if you are interested in the mechanics behind meals.

This UI feels a bit to much packed to my taste. However, there is a lot of information that the player needs to easily prepare a meal. We wanted this feature to enhance the gaming experience and be as intuitive to use as possible. All in all, we are fairly satisfied with the result.

We decided to have just 5 (by adding 1 to 5 berries to the pot) so that players can instinctively cook the meal they need, rather than spending several minutes looking for the right recipe from over 100 (as in Pokémon Scarlet and Violet).

For example, if the player needs to recover some HP and revive one Pokémon, all they have to do is browse through the available berries and choose the ones that will enable them to achieve just that. This way, the player only has to do a minimum of thinking, just knowing what they need out of the meal is already enough.

In this user interface, we have taken care to include all the information the player needs to prepare the meal they require (from top to bottom):

  • The number of berries needed for this meal.
  • Berries already selected.
  • For each meal statistic (HP recovered, PP recovered, status effects healed, and Pokémon resurrected):
    • The current statistic, in dark blue for the progress bar and side text.
    • The expected stastatistictus if you choose the berry currently highlighted, in light blue for the progress bar and side text.
  • For the current highlighted berry:
    • The number of berries of this type in the player's bag.
    • Statistical values for the berry in each category.
